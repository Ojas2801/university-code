Bachelor's degree

A bachelor's degree is an undergraduate academic degree typically awarded by colleges and universities, requiring three to four years of full-time study. Bachelor's degrees are offered in various disciplines, including arts and humanities, business and management, engineering, health sciences, natural sciences, social sciences, computer science and IT, education, nursing, and many more.

Master's degree

A master's degree is an advanced academic degree that typically requires one to two years of study beyond a bachelor's degree, providing specialized knowledge and skills in a specific field. It encompasses various types, including Master of Arts (MA), Master of Science (MS), Master of Business Administration (MBA), Master of Engineering (MEng), and Master of Fine Arts (MFA), among others.

Doctor of Philosophy (PhD)

A Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.) degree is the highest academic distinction, representing the pinnacle of achievement in a specific field. Typically requiring 3-6 years of study beyond a master's degree, a Ph.D. program emphasizes original research, critical thinking, and expertise. It involves advanced coursework, comprehensive exams, and the creation and defense of an original dissertation that contributes significantly to the field.
